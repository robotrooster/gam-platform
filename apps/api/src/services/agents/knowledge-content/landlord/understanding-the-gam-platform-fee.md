title: Understanding the GAM platform fee
---
GAM is the software that runs your rental business, and the platform fee is what keeps it running. Here's exactly how it's calculated, so there are no surprises.

The fee has two simple parts:

- $2 per occupied unit, per month
- A $10 per-property minimum, per month

The per-unit charge only applies to units that are actually occupied. Vacant units are never charged. So if you own a 6-unit property and 4 units are leased, you're billed for those 4 units that month, not all 6.

The per-property minimum is a floor, not an add-on. For each property, GAM compares your occupied-unit total against the $10 minimum and bills whichever is higher. Example: a property with 3 occupied units would calculate to $6 in per-unit fees ($2 x 3), so the $10 minimum applies instead. A property with 8 occupied units calculates to $16, which is above the floor, so you're billed $16. You're never charged both on top of each other.

The platform fee is separate from the processing fees on tenant payments. Those are covered in our article on payment processing fees and who pays them.

A few good things to know:

- Because vacant units aren't billed, your platform cost naturally tracks your occupancy.
- The fee covers the whole platform: rent collection, maintenance routing, payouts, reporting, and tenant communication.

If your billed amount ever looks off, the most common reason is a unit's occupancy status. Check that your units are marked correctly as occupied or vacant, and the math should line up.
