title: How Tenant Payment Fees & Pass-Through Work
---
When a tenant pays through GAM, a small payment-processing fee applies based on how they pay. A per-property setting decides whether the **tenant** or the **landlord** covers that fee. GAM never absorbs it.

## The fee structure

- **Bank account (ACH):** 1.0%, capped at $6.00 per payment.
- **Card:** 3.25% flat.
- **Canadian cards charged in USD:** add 1.5%.

These are processing fees on the payment itself — separate from the monthly platform fee for using GAM.

## Who pays the fee (pass-through)

Each property has a setting that determines who covers the payment fee:

- **Tenant pays:** the fee is passed through to the tenant on top of what they owe.
- **Landlord pays:** the fee is deducted on your side, so the tenant pays only the charge amount.

You choose this per property. Because bank/ACH payments are capped at $6.00 while card payments are a flat percentage, ACH is generally the lower-cost way to pay.

## A note on rent and what's owed

GAM does not set the rent — you set the rent amount, due date, grace period, and late fees in each lease. Payments are electronic only; cash paid outside the platform stays "unpaid" in GAM until the tenant pays electronically.

If you have a question about a specific fee charged on a payment, or anything involving moving or refunding money, we'll connect you with a human GAM specialist.
