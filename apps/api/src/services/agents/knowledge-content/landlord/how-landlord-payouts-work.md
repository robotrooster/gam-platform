title: Getting paid: how your payouts work
---
When a tenant pays rent through GAM, the money doesn't sit with us and wait. GAM is the software platform that moves the payment — it lands in your own connected Stripe account, and your payouts go out from there to your bank.

Here's the rhythm. Card and bank (ACH) payments take a little time to clear before the funds are truly available, so there's a short settling window after a tenant pays. Once funds clear, they show up as your available balance.

Payouts run on a regular schedule — a weekday batch, normally Friday. When Friday is a US federal holiday, the run shifts to the next available business day so a holiday never silently skips your money. On each run, GAM looks at the cleared, available balance in your connected account and sends it to your linked bank automatically. You don't have to push a button.

Standard bank payouts are free and typically arrive in one to two business days after they're sent. If you'd rather not wait for the next scheduled run, you can also request an on-demand withdrawal of your available balance from your banking page. An instant option is available where eligible — Stripe charges a small fee (1.5%, minimum $0.50) for instant transfers, which is deducted from the transfer itself.

A couple of things worth knowing. The platform fee is $2 per occupied unit each month plus a $10-per-property monthly minimum; vacant units are never charged. And whether the tenant or you cover the payment processing fee is set per property — with separate toggles for ACH and card — and you control it; GAM never absorbs that fee. Set rent, due dates, and grace periods on each lease, and the cleared rent flows to you on schedule. Payments are electronic only — if a tenant pays you in cash outside the platform, that charge stays marked "unpaid" in GAM until they pay electronically.
