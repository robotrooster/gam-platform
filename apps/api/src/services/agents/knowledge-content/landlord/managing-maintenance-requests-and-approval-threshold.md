title: Managing Maintenance Requests & the Approval Threshold
---
When a tenant has an issue with a unit — repairs, locks, appliances, plumbing, heat or cooling, pests, or damage — they file a **maintenance request** in GAM. That request is routed to you, the landlord. GAM is the platform that delivers and tracks the request; GAM does not perform repairs. The property and any repairs are your responsibility.

## How requests reach you

As the landlord, you receive each maintenance request your tenants submit and decide how to handle it. You own the decisions about what gets repaired, who does the work, and when.

## The approval threshold

GAM lets you set a maintenance **approval threshold** so you stay in control of spending. By default this is **$500**.

- Work estimated **at or below** your threshold can proceed without an extra approval step.
- Work estimated **above** your threshold is held for your approval before it moves forward.

You can adjust the threshold to fit how hands-on you want to be. A lower threshold means more items wait for your sign-off; a higher one lets more routine work proceed automatically.

## Tenant expectations

Because repairs and timelines are the landlord's call, what's required and how quickly depends on your lease and local law. GAM doesn't set repair obligations. If a tenant or you have a question that's legal in nature — such as habitability or required response times — we can connect you with a human GAM specialist.
