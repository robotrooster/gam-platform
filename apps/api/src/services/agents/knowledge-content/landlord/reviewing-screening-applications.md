title: Reviewing applicant screening submissions
---
When an applicant submits a screening application for one of your units, it shows up in your screening list in the portal. This article covers what you see and how to read it.

## Where applications appear

Your screening list shows every application tied to your account, newest first. Each row carries the applicant's name, the property and unit they applied to (when they applied to a specific one), and the current status of the check.

Opening an application shows the full submission the applicant provided:

- Name and date of birth
- The last four digits of their SSN (the full number is never shown to you — it is encrypted and handed only to the screening provider)
- Current address and how long they have lived there
- Employment status, employer name and phone, and monthly income
- Previous landlord name, phone, and email
- Any ID document and income documents they uploaded
- Which consents they agreed to (credit and criminal)

## The risk indicator

Each submission also carries a risk score and level. This is an intake-fraud signal only — it looks at things like disposable email addresses, suspicious SSN patterns, and submission velocity. It is **not** a credit or criminal assessment and is not a recommendation to approve or decline. Treat it as a heads-up to look closer, not a decision.

## Uploaded documents

ID and income documents the applicant attached open from the application detail. Access is locked to you (the owning landlord) and the applicant — no one else can pull those files.

## A note on access

Reviewing screening submissions requires the background-check permission. If you manage a team, only members you have granted that permission can see applicant data.

For what the statuses mean and how reports arrive, see the article on ordering and reading background checks. For making the call, see approving or declining an applicant.
