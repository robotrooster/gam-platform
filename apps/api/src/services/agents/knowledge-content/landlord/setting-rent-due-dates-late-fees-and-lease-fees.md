---
scope: landlord
title: Setting rent, due dates, late fees, and lease fees
---
You're in control of the money side of every lease. GAM doesn't set rent or decide what to charge — you do, per lease. Here's what you can configure.

Rent and due date. Set the monthly rent amount and the due date on the lease. GAM bills the tenant on that schedule. Payments are electronic only — tenants pay by ACH (1.0%, capped at $6) or card (3.25%; Canadian cards paying in USD add 1.5%). A per-property setting decides whether the tenant or you cover that processing fee — GAM never absorbs it.

Late fees. Late fees are fully optional and tunable per lease. You set:

- A grace period (number of days after the due date before a fee applies).
- An initial late fee, as either a flat dollar amount or a percent of rent.
- An optional recurring accrual (a flat or percent amount that repeats daily, weekly, or monthly while rent stays unpaid).
- An optional cap so the total late fee never exceeds a limit you choose.

If you turn on accrual, set all three pieces (amount, type, and period) or none — a half-configured rule won't apply.

Lease fees by timing. Beyond rent, you can attach fees to a lease and tell GAM when each one applies:

- Move-in fees (like the security deposit) are billed up front as part of the move-in bundle.
- Monthly fees recur alongside rent.
- Move-out fees (like a cleaning fee) come into play at lease end and can be settled from the deposit.

You can also bill a one-off fee against a tenant mid-lease for things like an early-termination charge or a negotiated cost.

Deposit amounts, refund rules, and anything legal-adjacent depend on your lease and your local laws — set them to fit your situation and check locally if you're unsure.
