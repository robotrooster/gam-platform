---
scope: landlord
title: Inviting a tenant to a unit
---
Once a unit exists, you can invite the person who'll live there. The invite creates their tenant account on GAM and gives them a private link to finish setting up.

To send an invite, enter the tenant's email, their first name, and the unit you're inviting them to. GAM creates a tenant account tied to your portfolio and generates a one-time acceptance link. That link is good for 7 days, so let the tenant know to act on it promptly; if it lapses, just send a fresh invite. Throwaway or temporary email addresses are blocked, since the tenant needs a real inbox to verify their account and receive notices.

A couple of things worth understanding about how this works. The invite stands a tenant up as an account holder — it does not, by itself, place them on a lease or formally assign them to the unit. The actual unit assignment happens through the lease you send for signature. So the usual flow is: invite the tenant, they accept and create their login, and then you send the lease through GAM's e-signing for both sides to sign.

The lease is where the real terms live. You set the rent amount, the due date, any grace period, and how late fees work — all per lease. GAM doesn't set those numbers; you do, and they should reflect your lease and what's allowed under your local rules.

If you have several units ready, you can invite each tenant the same way. Once a tenant accepts and their lease is signed, the unit flips from vacant to occupied, payments can start flowing, and they'll be able to file maintenance requests that route straight to you.
