title: Adding your properties and units to GAM
---
Getting your portfolio into GAM starts with adding a property, then filling it with units. A property is the building or community itself; units are the individual rentable spaces inside it.

To add a property, give it a name and address and choose its type (for example a standard residential building or an RV community). As part of setup you'll decide, for each payment method, who covers the processing fee on that property. ACH payments run 1.0% (capped at $6), cards run 3.25%, and a card paid on a Canadian card in USD adds 1.5%. A per-property setting decides whether the tenant or you absorb each fee — GAM never covers it. You can revisit who pays the ACH and card fees later without rebuilding the property.

This is also where you connect the bank account your payouts land in. GAM moves money through Stripe Connect, so you'll go through Stripe's onboarding (identity and banking verification, known as KYC) before payouts can flow. Funds reach you on a schedule once payments clear.

Once the property exists, add units. The fastest path is to create them in bulk: pick a unit type, a count, and an optional naming prefix, and GAM numbers them for you (RV 01, RV 02, and so on). New units start as vacant, and you're only billed the platform fee on occupied units — $2 per occupied unit per month, with a $10 per-property monthly minimum. Vacant units cost nothing.

With properties and units in place, you're ready to list vacancies and bring tenants on board.
