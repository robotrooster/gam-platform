scope: landlord
title: Approving or declining an applicant
---
Once a background check is complete, you make the call. GAM records your decision and notifies the applicant — but the decision itself is always yours.

## When you can decide

You can approve or decline a check once it has results back from the provider — that is, when its status is **complete** (or while it is still **processing** or **submitted**, if you choose to act early). You cannot record a decision on a check that is pending fraud-screening, failed, cancelled, or expired; those have to resolve first.

## Recording the decision

From the application detail, choose **Approve** or **Decline**. You can attach notes explaining your reasoning. Once recorded:

- The decision and timestamp are saved against the check, along with who decided it.
- The applicant is emailed the outcome, including any notes you added.
- An **approval** marks the check valid for six months, so you have a window to move toward a lease without re-screening.

## After a decline

When you decline an applicant whose check ran through a screening provider, GAM automatically generates and emails the applicant the required decline notice on your behalf, and keeps a copy on file for the check. This is the kind of step that is legal-adjacent: exactly what is required, what it must say, and any waiting periods depend on your lease terms and your local laws. GAM handles the mechanical notice; making sure your overall process meets your obligations is on you — check your local laws or your own counsel if you are unsure.

## Re-deciding

If you revisit a check, GAM will not send a duplicate decline notice — the original notice stays the legal record on file.

## What approval does not do

Approving a screening check does not create a lease or move anyone in. It clears the applicant in your screening records. The lease itself, including rent amount, due date, grace period, and any fees, is set up separately when you build and send the lease. See the leasing articles for that next step.
