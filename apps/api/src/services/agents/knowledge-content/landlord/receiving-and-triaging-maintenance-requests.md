title: Receiving and triaging maintenance requests (and the approval threshold)
---
When a tenant files a maintenance request, it lands in your maintenance list automatically. GAM doesn't do the repairs itself — we route the request to you so you can decide what happens next. Each request shows the unit, property, what the tenant reported, any photos they attached, and how many comments are on the thread.

Your list sorts itself by priority so the urgent stuff floats to the top. Emergencies come first, then high, then normal, then low — followed by newest within each group. That ordering means you can open your maintenance view and immediately see what needs attention today versus what can wait.

From there you can leave comments on the request. Comments can be visible to the tenant or marked internal — internal notes stay between you and your team and are never shown to the tenant, which is handy for jotting down vendor quotes or scheduling thoughts before you've made a decision.

The approval threshold is the safeguard around cost. You set a dollar amount (the default is $500, and it's configurable in your settings) above which a job needs your explicit sign-off before work proceeds. Here's how it kicks in: when an estimated cost is entered on a request and that estimate is over your threshold, the request automatically flips to "awaiting approval" instead of moving straight ahead. Tenants don't see this internal approval step — it's just for you. Estimates at or below the threshold don't trigger it and keep moving normally.

Think of the threshold as the line between "handle it" and "check with me first." Set it where you're comfortable letting routine work flow without a second look, while still catching the bigger expenses before anyone commits.
