title: Assigning work, tracking cost, and marking maintenance complete
---
Once you've triaged a request, GAM walks it through a simple lifecycle: open, then assigned, in progress, and finally completed (or cancelled). Each step updates the request, drops a status note onto the thread, and notifies the tenant so they're never left wondering what's happening with their issue.

To move a job forward, assign it to whoever's handling the repair. Assigning stamps the request with the assignment time and flips it to "assigned." As the work gets going, you can mark it "in progress," and the tenant gets an update at each stage. GAM is the software layer here — we keep the record and the notifications flowing, but the actual repair coordination is yours.

For cost, you can record an estimated cost and an actual cost. The estimate is what feeds the approval threshold: if it's over your configured limit (default $500), the request waits in "awaiting approval" until you approve it. Approving sends it on to the assigned worker if one is already set, or back to open if not, and the tenant is notified that their request is moving forward. The actual cost is what you log once the work is done.

When the repair is finished, mark the request "completed." GAM stamps the completion time and, if you've entered an actual cost, notes it on the thread. A platform fee applies to completed maintenance based on the actual cost recorded.

Your dashboard rolls all of this up: counts of open, assigned, in-progress, and completed work, a flag for any open emergencies, and totals for cost and fees on completed jobs — so you always have a clear read on what's outstanding and what you've spent.
