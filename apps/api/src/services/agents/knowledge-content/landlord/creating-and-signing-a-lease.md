scope: landlord
title: Creating and signing a lease
---
A lease in GAM ties a tenant to a specific unit and holds all the terms you've agreed on: who's renting, the unit, the start and end dates, the rent, and any fees. You build one through Tenant Onboarding, which walks you from drafting the terms to collecting signatures.

Here's how it flows:

- Draft the lease. Pick the unit and the tenant, choose the lease type (a fixed-term lease needs an end date; a month-to-month lease doesn't have one), and set the rent and any fees. While the lease is still a draft, you can edit everything freely.
- Send it for signing. GAM's e-sign step collects signatures from you and the tenant. You can add more than one tenant to a lease — one is the primary, others are additional signers.
- It goes active. Once it's signed, the lease is live, the unit shows as occupied, and rent billing follows the schedule you set.

A few things worth knowing once a lease is active:

- Rent and term changes (the rent amount, dates, or lease type) can't be edited in place. Those need a brand-new lease with new signatures that replaces the old one — start that from Tenant Onboarding.
- Smaller changes (late-fee settings, notice periods, the deposit) update the lease in place and create an addendum record on the tenant's history. GAM asks you to confirm before applying so you know it's being logged.

GAM is the software that holds and manages the lease — the terms, the deposit rules, and anything legal-adjacent are up to you and depend on your lease and your local laws. When in doubt, check with a local professional before you finalize.
