title: How to file a maintenance request
---
When something in your home needs attention — a leaky faucet, a broken appliance, a heating issue — you can file a maintenance request right from your GAM account. Here's how it works.

Go to the Maintenance section and create a new request — it's automatically filed against your unit, so there's nothing to select. You'll fill in:

- A short title (for example, "Kitchen sink dripping")
- A description with as much detail as you can give — what's wrong, when it started, anything you've already tried
- A priority: emergency, high, normal, or low
- Photos, if you have them — a picture often explains the problem faster than words

Once you submit, your request is sent straight to your landlord (or their maintenance team). GAM is the software platform that carries the request and keeps everyone in sync — we don't perform the repairs ourselves, and the landlord decides who does the work and when.

A quick word on emergencies. If you're facing something that threatens safety or could cause serious damage right now — a fire, a gas smell, flooding, no heat in dangerous cold — do not wait on an app. Call emergency services first, then contact your landlord directly using the emergency number they've given you. Filing in GAM is the right move for everything that can safely wait for a normal response, and it creates a clear, time-stamped record of what you reported.

The more specific your description and photos, the faster your landlord can size up the job and get the right person out. After you submit, you'll be able to follow the request as it moves along — see our article on checking the status of a request.
