title: Landlord entry requests: how notice and your response work
---
Sometimes your landlord needs to come into your unit, for example to make a repair, run an inspection, or show the space. GAM handles this through entry requests, so there's a clear, time-stamped record on both sides instead of a phone call you have to remember.

When your landlord creates a request, you'll get a notification with the reason, the category (maintenance, inspection, showing, emergency, or other), and a proposed entry window with a start and end time. The request also records how much advance notice you're being given, measured against the notice window your landlord has set up. Emergencies are handled differently from planned visits.

You can respond directly in your tenant portal by choosing granted or denied, and you can add a short note explaining your answer. Responding promptly helps your landlord plan and keeps the record clean. You can only respond while the request is still pending; if it's already been cancelled or acted on, you won't be able to change it.

After the visit, your landlord records the actual time they entered. You'll get a notification confirming the entry was logged, and the request closes out. Every request, response, and recorded entry stays visible in your portal so you always have the history.

How much notice your landlord must give, and your rights around granting or refusing entry, depend on your lease and your local law. GAM is the software platform that records and routes these requests; it doesn't set the rules for them. If you're unsure what notice you're entitled to or how to handle a request, review your lease first and reach out to your landlord through the platform with any questions.
