title: Understanding your lease in GAM
---
Your lease is the agreement between you and your landlord, and GAM is the platform that holds it and keeps everything organized in one place. GAM isn't your landlord — your landlord sets the terms, and we make them easy to find and act on.

To view your lease, sign in to your tenant portal and open the Leases section. You'll see each lease you're a part of, with the property name and your unit number right at the top. Open one to see the full picture: your rent amount, the move-in and (if it's a fixed-term lease) end date, your security deposit, and any other fees attached to the lease, along with the other people named on it.

A few things worth knowing about who controls what. Your rent amount, your due date, any grace period before a payment counts as late, and how late fees work are all set by your landlord on your specific lease. If a number looks off, your landlord is the one who can change it — GAM just displays what's on file.

Rent is paid electronically through GAM, by bank transfer (ACH) or card. A small processing fee may apply, and a per-property setting decides whether you or your landlord covers it; if it's on you, you'll see it clearly before you confirm a payment.

If you can't find your lease under the Leases section, or a document looks out of date, let us know and we'll help you locate the right version or reach your landlord. And if you ever spot something that doesn't match what you agreed to, reach out to your landlord through your portal. Anything tied to your rights, deposit, or local requirements depends on your lease and the laws where you live, so check those alongside your written agreement.
