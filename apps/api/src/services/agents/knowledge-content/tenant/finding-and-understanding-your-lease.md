title: Finding and Understanding Your Lease
---
Your lease is the agreement between you and your landlord, and it's the source of truth for the terms of your tenancy. You can view it any time in your tenant portal.

## Where to find it

Sign in and open the **Documents** section of your portal. Your lease and related documents are stored there for you to read or download.

## What your lease covers

Your lease — set by your landlord — typically spells out things like:

- Your rent amount and when it's due
- Any grace period and late fees
- The length of your tenancy and renewal terms
- Rules for the property and your unit
- Deposit terms

These terms come from your landlord, not from GAM. GAM is the platform that stores and displays your lease and processes your payments; it does not set your rent or write your lease terms.

## Legal questions

For questions about your rights, deposits, notice periods, or anything legal-adjacent, the answer depends on your specific lease and the laws where you live. We'd suggest reviewing your lease first and checking your local laws, and we can connect you with a human GAM specialist for legal questions.

If you can't find your lease in Documents, or a document looks out of date, let us know and we'll help you locate the right version or reach your landlord.
