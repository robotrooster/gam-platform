title: How your security deposit works
---
Your security deposit is money you pay at the start of your lease that your landlord holds in case there are charges to settle when you move out. On GAM, the deposit is part of your lease terms: the amount is set by your landlord in the lease you sign, listed as a move-in item alongside your first month's rent and any other move-in fees.

## Who sets the amount

GAM is the software platform that runs your payments and your lease record. We don't set your deposit, charge it, or decide what it covers. Your landlord sets the deposit amount in the lease, and you can see it on your lease details before you sign. If the figure ever looks wrong to you, your landlord is the right person to ask, because they control that field.

## How it's paid and held

You pay your deposit electronically, the same way you pay rent on GAM, by bank transfer (ACH) or card. There's no cash or check option. Depending on a setting your landlord chooses for the property, a small processing fee may be added to your payment or covered by your landlord. The deposit is then held against your lease until move-out; it isn't applied to your monthly rent along the way.

## Deposit interest

In some places, a held deposit earns interest that's owed back to you, and the rules differ from one location to another. GAM tracks any interest that applies to your lease automatically and adds it to what you're owed at move-out. Whether interest applies at all, and how it's calculated, depends on your lease and your local laws, so check those for the specifics.

## At move-out

When your lease ends, your landlord reviews the deposit, applies any agreed-upon deductions, and either refunds the balance to you or, if charges exceed the deposit, bills the difference. See "Getting your deposit back at move-out" for how that settlement works step by step.

Your deposit and its activity are always visible in your GAM account, so you have a clear record from the day you pay it.
