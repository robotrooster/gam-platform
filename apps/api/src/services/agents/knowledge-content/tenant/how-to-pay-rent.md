scope: tenant
title: How to pay your rent in GAM
---
GAM is the platform your landlord uses to collect rent. Payments are electronic-only — there's no cash or check option inside GAM. You can pay one of two ways, using a payment method you've saved to your account.

**Bank account (ACH).** This is usually the lower-cost option. The fee is 1.0% of the payment, capped at $6.00 — so even on a large rent payment, the ACH fee never goes above six dollars. Before you can pay by bank, your account needs to be set up and your bank verified.

**Debit or credit card.** Cards are charged a flat 3.25%. If you're paying with a Canadian card in U.S. dollars, an extra 1.5% is added to cover the cross-border cost. Cards are convenient and confirm quickly, but the fee is higher than ACH on most rent amounts.

**Who pays the fee?** That depends on a setting your landlord chooses for your property. On some properties the tenant covers the processing fee; on others the landlord does. You'll see the exact amount and who's covering it before you confirm a payment, so there are no surprises.

The rent amount, due date, any grace period, and late fees are all set by your landlord on your lease — GAM doesn't set those. When a payment row shows as due, open it, pick your saved bank or card, review the total (rent plus any fee that applies to you), and confirm. That's it. If you have a question about the dollar amount itself or your due date, that's a conversation for your landlord.
