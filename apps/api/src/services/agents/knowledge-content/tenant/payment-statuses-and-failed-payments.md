title: Payment statuses and what happens if a payment fails
---
After you submit a rent payment in GAM, it moves through a few statuses. Here's what each one means.

**Pending** — the payment has been set up and is waiting to be paid. This is what you'll see for an upcoming charge before you've sent it.

**Processing** — your bank (ACH) payment is in flight. Bank transfers take a little time to clear, so an ACH payment sits in processing for a bit before it's confirmed. Card payments skip this step.

**Settled** — the money has cleared and the payment is complete. Card payments typically reach this almost right away; ACH payments get here once the bank transfer finishes.

**Failed / Returned** — the payment didn't go through. With bank payments, this usually means the transfer was returned by the bank (for example, insufficient funds or an account issue).

**What happens after a failure?** Most returned bank payments are simply retry-eligible — the charge stays open and you can try again, often with a different payment method. In some cases, depending on the reason the bank gives, GAM may pause ACH on your account and ask you to re-verify your bank before you can use it again; you can still pay by card in the meantime.

A failed payment means your rent hasn't been received yet, so it's worth resolving quickly. Keep in mind that grace periods and late fees are set by your landlord on your lease, not by GAM — so check your lease terms and reach out to your landlord if a failed payment might push you past your due date. Once you re-submit and it settles, you're all caught up.
