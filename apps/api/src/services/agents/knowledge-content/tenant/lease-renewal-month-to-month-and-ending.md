title: Renewal, month-to-month, and ending your lease
---
As your lease nears its end, a few different things can happen, and which one applies depends on how your landlord set up your lease in GAM. We'll walk through the options so you know what to expect.

Fixed-term leases have a set end date. Month-to-month leases don't — they simply continue period to period until someone ends them. You can see which type you have on your lease page in the tenant portal.

Auto-renew is a setting your landlord can turn on. When it's enabled, your lease renews automatically as the end date approaches instead of lapsing, often rolling into a new term or into month-to-month, depending on how it's configured. If auto-renew is off, the lease moves toward its natural end. Either way, your landlord usually provides advance notice before the end date, and the amount of notice is part of the lease terms.

Keep in mind that real changes to your lease — like a different rent amount or a new term — aren't edited in place. Those require a brand-new lease with new signatures, so you'll always get a chance to review and sign before anything binding takes effect.

If you need to leave before a fixed-term lease ends, you can request early termination from your lease page. GAM will first show you a quote for any fee your landlord has set, and you can submit or cancel the request. Your landlord may also choose to waive the fee.

Notice periods, deposit returns, and what's required to end a lease properly depend on your lease and the laws where you live, so review your written agreement and local rules. When in doubt, talk to your landlord through the portal.
