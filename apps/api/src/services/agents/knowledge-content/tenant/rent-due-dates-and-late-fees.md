title: How Rent Due Dates and Late Fees Are Set
---
Your rent amount, your due date, any grace period, and any late fees are all set by your **landlord** and written into your lease. GAM is the platform that displays these terms and processes your payments — it does not set your rent or decide your late fees.

## Where these terms live

You'll find your due date, grace period, and late-fee terms in your lease, which you can view in the **Lease** section of your tenant portal. Your payments area shows upcoming amounts and their due dates so you know what's coming.

## How late fees work

Late fees, including whether there's a grace period before one applies and how much it is, are defined by your landlord in your lease. If a late fee appears on your account, it reflects the terms your landlord configured — not a charge GAM adds on its own.

## Paying on time

Because GAM is electronic-only, the way to keep a payment from being late is to pay by bank account (ACH) or card through the platform before the due date. Keep in mind that bank (ACH) payments can take a few days to clear, so starting a payment a bit ahead of the due date helps it post in time.

## If a late fee looks wrong

First, check your lease to confirm the grace period and late-fee terms. If something still seems off, your landlord governs these terms — and for questions about whether a fee was applied correctly, or anything legal-adjacent, we can connect you with a human GAM specialist.
