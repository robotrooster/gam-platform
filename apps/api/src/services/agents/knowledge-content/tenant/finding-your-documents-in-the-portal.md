scope: tenant
title: Finding and viewing your documents in the portal
---
All of the documents tied to your tenancy live in one place in your GAM portal, so you don't have to dig through email to find a copy of your lease later. When your landlord sends you something to sign, it shows up in your documents area and you'll also get a notification (and an email) letting you know it's ready.

Things that are still waiting on your signature appear in a pending list, so it's easy to see at a glance what needs your attention. Each item shows the document title, the property and unit it relates to, and who sent it. Once you open a document, it's marked as viewed so your landlord knows it reached you.

After a document has been fully signed by everyone, it doesn't disappear. You can re-open it any time as a read-only copy and see the complete, finished version — including every field and signature, not just the parts you filled in. When the signed PDF has been generated, you can also download that executed copy for your own records. We'd encourage you to save a copy somewhere safe.

A quick note on access: documents are private to the people involved. Only the people listed as signers and the landlord on a document can open or download it — your information isn't visible to other tenants.

Keep in mind that GAM is the software platform your landlord uses, not the landlord itself. The terms inside any document — rent, dates, deposit, and policies — are set by your landlord. If something in a document doesn't look right, the best move is to reach out to your landlord directly before signing.
