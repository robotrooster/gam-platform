scope: tenant
title: Setting up bank (ACH) payments to pay your rent
---
Paying rent straight from your bank account (ACH) is the lowest-cost way to pay in GAM — the fee is 1% of the payment, capped at $6.00, so even on a large rent payment it never goes above six dollars. Paying by card works too, but it costs more (a flat 3.25%). So if you can, connecting a bank account is the way to go.

You set this up in the **Payments** section of your portal. To add a bank account, you connect it instantly: sign in to your bank through a secure connection and pick the account. There are no micro-deposits and no waiting — it verifies right away, so you can pay by bank immediately.

GAM only ever stores and shows the last four digits of the account — your full account and routing numbers aren't exposed in the portal.

Once your bank is connected and verified, you can pay rent by bank (ACH). Each cycle you go to the Payments section and pay the amount due from your connected bank — payment is something you initiate, not an automatic pull. If you're not sure whether your bank is set up yet, the support team can check your account and see whether a bank is connected and verified — and walk you to the right spot if it isn't.

A couple of things worth knowing: a bank (ACH) payment takes a little longer to clear than a card, and your bank needs enough funds when it's pulled, or the payment can be returned. If a payment is ever returned, that's handled through the banking system — the fix is to make sure the bank account is set up correctly and funded, then the next pull goes through.
