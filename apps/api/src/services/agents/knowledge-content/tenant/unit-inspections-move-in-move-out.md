title: Unit inspections: move-in, move-out, and what your signature means
---
GAM gives your landlord a structured way to document the condition of your unit, and gives you a clear record of what was found. There are three kinds of inspection: move-in (done around the start of your tenancy), move-out (done when you leave), and periodic (a routine check during your stay).

Your landlord schedules the inspection and walks through the unit area by area. Each item, like a wall, an appliance, or flooring, gets a condition: good, fair, damaged, missing, or not applicable. They can attach photos and notes to back up what they recorded. When the documentation is ready, you'll get a notification asking you to review and sign.

What does signing mean? Your signature is your attestation that what you're seeing matches what's documented. It does not mean you agree the unit is perfect, and it does not waive anything; it confirms the record reflects what's actually there. Look over every item and photo carefully before you sign. If something looks wrong or is missing, contact your landlord through the platform before signing so it can be corrected, since items can't be edited once the inspection is finalized.

After you sign, your landlord signs to finalize, and the inspection is locked. At move-out, GAM compares your move-out inspection against your move-in record for the same items, so anything that got worse is documented side by side. Brand-new items not on the move-in list don't count against that comparison.

You can view all of your inspections and their results anytime in your tenant portal. How any documented damage affects deposits or charges depends on your lease and your local law, so review your lease for those details.
