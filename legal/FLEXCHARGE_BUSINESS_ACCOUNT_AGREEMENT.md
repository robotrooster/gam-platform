## 6. Optional ACH Auto-Pay Authorization

The Account Holder may, at the Account Holder's election,
authorize the Platform to initiate Automated Clearing House
(ACH) debits from the Account Holder's designated bank account
to satisfy amounts owed on the FlexCharge account as they come
due. **ACH auto-pay is optional and is initiated only with the
Account Holder's express authorization at the Platform's
payment-method-setup surface.**

If the Account Holder elects ACH auto-pay, the following terms
apply:

- The Platform, acting through Stripe, Inc., will initiate an
  ACH debit on each Payment Due Date in the amount then due
  under the Payment Schedule in Section 4.
- ACH debits route through the Platform's payment rail; on
  successful settlement, funds are routed to the Creditor's
  Connect account in accordance with GAM's payment-routing
  policy (Consumer ToS § 5.5 and § 9.1.3). The Account Holder
  acknowledges that the Platform's GAM-First Payment Routing
  applies to all ACH pulls authorized through the Platform,
  and a portion of any successful pull may be applied to
  outstanding GAM balances before being applied to the
  Creditor's FlexCharge balance.
- **Failed ACH pulls.** If an ACH debit fails (insufficient
  funds, account closed, or any other Nacha return code),
  the Platform will retry under the failed-ACH retry policy
  described in Consumer ToS § 5.4. The retry amount on
  FlexCharge is the **same underlying amount** as the original
  pull (FlexCharge is a fixed-balance account, not a date-
  formula product), plus any Stripe ACH-return fees passed
  through at GAM's actual cost. Stripe pass-through is not a
  finance charge under 12 C.F.R. § 1026.4 and is in addition
  to any late fee the Creditor may charge under Section 4.
- The Account Holder may revoke ACH auto-pay authorization at
  any time through the Platform's payment-method-management
  surface, with revocation taking effect on the next
  scheduled debit. Revocation does not relieve the Account
  Holder of the obligation to satisfy the underlying
  FlexCharge balance owed to the Creditor under the Payment
  Schedule; it only stops automatic collection through the
  Platform.

If the Account Holder does not elect ACH auto-pay, the Account
Holder is responsible for making payments to the Creditor
through any other payment method the Creditor accepts on the
Platform (card, in-person at the POS, or other Creditor-
configured methods).

## 7. Default and Collection

Default on the FlexCharge account, and the consequences of
default, are governed by the terms the Creditor configures in
Section 4 and any additional default-and-collection terms the
Creditor presents to the Account Holder at the time of charge
or on the Account Holder's Platform statement. Default may
include, at the Creditor's election: suspension of further
charging privileges; application of late fees and any
finance charge configured under Section 4; referral to the
Creditor's collections process; and, where the Account Holder
is a Tenant under Section 2, separate consequences under the
lease only to the extent the lease expressly so provides.

**GAM is not the creditor and is not engaged in the collection
of any FlexCharge balance.** Any third-party collection
activity engaged by the Creditor is the Creditor's
responsibility, including compliance with the Fair Debt
Collection Practices Act (15 U.S.C. § 1692 et seq.) and any
state debt-collection law. The optional GAM Collections
Partner integration described in Consumer ToS § 5.6 is for
**unpaid rent only** and is **not engaged on any FlexCharge
balance**.

If the Account Holder believes a charge posted to the
FlexCharge account is in error, the Account Holder should:

1. **First, raise the dispute with the Creditor directly**
   through the Platform's dispute-routing surface or at the
   Location. The Creditor is the party responsible for
   investigating and resolving billing errors on the
   FlexCharge account under the Fair Credit Billing Act (15
   U.S.C. § 1666 et seq.) and any applicable state-law
   equivalent.
2. If the dispute is not resolved with the Creditor, the
   Account Holder may submit a dispute through the Platform's
   dispute-management surface; GAM will route the dispute
   record to the Creditor and surface the Creditor's response
   to the Account Holder, but GAM does not adjudicate, decide,
   or guarantee any outcome on FlexCharge disputes.

The Account Holder retains all rights and remedies available
under applicable law against the Creditor as the creditor on
the FlexCharge account.

## 10. Term and Termination

This Agreement takes effect on the Effective Date and
continues until terminated as follows:

- **By the Account Holder**: at any time, by written notice
  to the Creditor through the Platform's dispute-and-cancel
  surface or at the Location. Termination by the Account
  Holder does not relieve the Account Holder of the obligation
  to pay any outstanding FlexCharge balance owed to the
  Creditor at the time of termination, in accordance with the
  Payment Schedule in Section 4.
- **By the Creditor**: at any time, with notice to the
  Account Holder as required by applicable law and the Payment
  Schedule.
- **By GAM (software termination only)**: GAM may terminate
  the software services described in Section 9 at any time on
  notice to both parties. Termination of GAM's software
  services does not by itself terminate the credit
  relationship between the Creditor and the Account Holder;
  the parties remain responsible for any FlexCharge balance
  owed at the time of termination, and the Creditor remains
  responsible for billing, statementing, and collecting the
  balance through other means.
- **By operation of the per-Location enablement**: if the
  Creditor disables FlexCharge at the Location, this Agreement
  terminates as to future charges, but any FlexCharge balance
  outstanding at the time of disablement remains owed to the
  Creditor and may be collected by the Creditor through other
  means.
