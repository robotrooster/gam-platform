# Session 85 Handoff

**Theme:** Item 19 — email systems consolidation. Two parallel email
paths (services/email.ts on Resend, lib/email.ts on nodemailer) collapsed
to a single Resend sender. nodemailer + @types/nodemailer dropped from
the dependency graph.

## Architecture decision recorded

Single sender: **Resend**. All transactional mail now flows through
`apps/api/src/services/email.ts`. The base/p/h/btn helpers in that
file are the canonical email building blocks — new templates compose
from them, no inline `<style>` or one-off transports.

`apps/api/src/lib/email.ts` was deleted, not merged. Recon found zero
consumers anywhere in the codebase — the 4 nodemailer functions had
been declared but never wired into any route, scheduler, or job. They
were templates encoding product intent for flows that haven't shipped.
The templates themselves were ported to services/email.ts (signatures
preserved) so the next session that wires them in doesn't have to
reinvent the copy or the visual design.

Why Resend won: it's the modern path, has 11 of 15 templates already
on it, has actual production usage (the e-sign flow and the invitation
flow both ship), and its npm audit posture is cleaner than nodemailer's.
